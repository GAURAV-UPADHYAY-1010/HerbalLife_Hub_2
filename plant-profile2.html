Abelmoschus manihot (L.) Medik.

Abelmoschus manihot (L.) Medik.

Common Name: Velvet Leaf, Red Okra

Family: MALVACEAE

Medicinal Properties:

Abelmoschus manihot is traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. It helps manage conditions like arthritis, protects cells from oxidative stress, and may have antibacterial effects.

Cultivation

Climate:

Warm tropical and subtropical regions are ideal for its cultivation.

Soil: Prefers well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.0-6.8.

Planting: Sow seeds directly into the soil after frost or start indoors.

Watering: Regular watering is essential but avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer during the growing season.

Harvesting

Timing:

Harvest leaves throughout the growing season and roots at the end of the season when mature.

Uses:

Abelmoschus manihot leaves and roots can be used in medicinal preparations like decoctions, tinctures, and topical applications.
