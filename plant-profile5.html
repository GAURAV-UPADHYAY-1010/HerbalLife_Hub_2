Abroma augusta (L.) L.f.

Abroma augusta (L.) L.f.

Common Name: Ulatkambal

Family: MALVACEAE

Medicinal Properties:

Anti-inflammatory, Antioxidant, Antimicrobial, Aphrodisiac, Diuretic.

Cultivation

Climate:

Warm, tropical regions with ample sunlight and high humidity.

Soil: Prefers fertile, well-drained soil with a pH of 6.0-7.0.

Planting: Propagate through seeds or cuttings. Plant in pots or directly in the ground and transplant when ready.

Watering: Regular watering, but avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Fertilizing: Apply balanced fertilizer as needed during the growing season.

Harvesting

Leaves:

Harvest throughout the growing season as needed.

Bark:

Harvest from mature trees.

Roots:

Dig up the roots carefully at the end of the growing season.

Usage Forms:

Used in various forms such as decoctions, tinctures, and topical applications. Consult a healthcare professional before use.
