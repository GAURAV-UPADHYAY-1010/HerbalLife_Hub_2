Acacia catechu (L.f.) Willd.

Common Names:

Indian catechu, Khair, Cutch tree

Family: FABACEAE (Leguminosae)

Medicinal Uses:

  • Antidiarrheal properties: Tannins in the heartwood reduce diarrhea.
  • Antioxidant effects: Protect cells from damage.
  • Antimicrobial activity: Antibacterial and antifungal properties.
  • Oral health: Used in mouthwashes and toothpastes.
  • Other applications: Treats skin conditions, ulcers, and dysentery.

Cultivation Methods:

Climate:

Thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with ample sunlight.

Soil:

Prefers well-drained, fertile soil with slightly acidic pH.

Planting:

Propagate through seeds or cuttings; sow directly or transplant cuttings.

Spacing:

Plant seedlings or cuttings 10-15 feet apart.

Watering:

Water regularly, especially during dry periods. Avoid overwatering.

Fertilizing:

Apply balanced fertilizer as needed during the growing season.

Pruning:

Regular pruning is needed to maintain shape and promote growth.

Harvesting:

Heartwood: Harvested from mature trees by making incisions to extract sap, which is dried and processed into catechu.

Note: While Acacia catechu has traditional medicinal uses, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies. Excessive consumption can have adverse effects on health.
