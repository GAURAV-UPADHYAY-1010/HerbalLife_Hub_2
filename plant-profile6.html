Abrus precatorius L.

Abrus precatorius L.

Common Name: Ratti, Ghungchi

Family: FABACEAE

Medicinal Properties:

Skin conditions, Eye diseases, Respiratory disorders, Fever, Rheumatism, Snakebites.

Note: Use with extreme caution as the plant is highly toxic due to the presence of abrin.

Cultivation

Climate:

Thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with ample sunlight.

Soil: Prefers fertile, well-drained soil with a slightly acidic pH.

Planting: Propagate through seeds. Sow directly into the soil after the last frost.

Watering: Regular watering is required, especially during dry periods.

Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer as needed during the growing season.

Harvesting

Seeds:

Harvest the mature seed pods when they turn brown and dry.

Important: The seeds are highly toxic. Handle with care, wear gloves, and avoid ingestion.

Caution: Abrus precatorius is extremely toxic, and ingestion of even a small amount can be fatal. Avoid using this plant for medicinal purposes. Seek medical help immediately if poisoning is suspected.
