Abutilon hirtum (Lam.) Sweet

Abutilon hirtum (Lam.) Sweet

Common Names: Indian mallow, Velvetleaf, Country mallow,Barakanghi

Family: MALVACEAE

Medicinal Properties:

Anti-inflammatory, Antioxidant, Diuretic, Antimicrobial, Treats respiratory disorders, Used for skin conditions.

Cultivation

Climate:

Thrives in warm, tropical climates with ample sunlight.

Soil: Prefers well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.0-7.0.

Planting: Propagate through seeds or cuttings. Sow seeds directly into the soil or plant cuttings in pots and transplant them once established.

Watering: Regular watering is required, especially during dry periods. Avoid overwatering.

Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer as needed during the growing season.

Pruning: Prune regularly to maintain shape and encourage growth.

Harvesting

Leaves:

Harvest leaves throughout the growing season as needed.

Bark:

Harvest bark from mature trees.

Roots:

Carefully dig up the roots at the end of the growing season.

Note: Abutilon hirtum is used in various preparations, including decoctions, tinctures, and topical applications. Always consult with a healthcare professional before use.
